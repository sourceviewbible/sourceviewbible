The Messiah will be the son of Judah

Gen 49:8-12; Luk 3:23-33


The words from Genesis record Jacob's final blessing over his fourth son, Judah. They speak of one of his descendants who will be a rightful ruler and will be honored by all nations. The genealogy in Luke's gospel starts with Jesus and works back through Joseph's line 77 generations to the beginning of creation. It lists Judah (son of Jacob) as Jesus' 51-times great grandfather.

REFLECTION & APPLICATION

  • It was highly unusual that the fourth son (not the first-born) would be declared to be the one through whom the regal dynastic responsibilities would be reckoned. This was certainly a special arrangement. What else do you find to be special about Jesus' birth?
  • Luke says that Jesus “was known as the son of Joseph.” Reflect on what is implied or hinted at by this unusual phrase in light of the story found in Luke 1:26-38.
  • The genealogy ends describing Jesus as “the son of Adam ... the son of God.” What does this tell you about Jesus' unique identity? How do you respond to this insight?
  • Jesus, Judah's heir, is to be honored by all. Why is this an appropriate response to his coming? How can you set your heart to honor him during this Advent season?
